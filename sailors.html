What we look for

We work with experienced sailors

Olive Crew places seafarers who already hold sea time, valid certificates and a working record we can check — mostly for bulk carrier positions, plus tankers and container ships. We don't currently place cadets or first-contract sailors without prior sea service.

FAQ

Questions sailors ask us

How much does it cost to get placed?

Free of charge for the sailor — the shipowner reimburses costs from your first salary.

How long does it take to find a vacancy?

It depends on your rank and vessel type, but a recruiter typically responds within one business day of your application.

What documents do I need to apply?

Your seaman's book, STCW certificates and medical book are the baseline — your recruiter will confirm the exact list for your rank.

Do you take on cadets without experience?

No — we currently place experienced seafarers only, with prior sea service and valid certificates. We don't take on cadets or first-time applicants.

Real talk

What sailors actually ask when something goes wrong

Real questions in the way sailors phrase them, with the honest, step-by-step answer — not a generic "contact us."

Asked by sailors
"My rotation keeps getting pushed back and nobody's telling me why. Can they just keep me out here?"

Straight answer: Your contract has a fixed term, and you're entitled to file a repatriation letter at least 30 days before you want to sign off. That said, sign-off depends on where the vessel actually is — if you're mid-ocean when your term ends, you wait for the next port, not because anyone's ignoring you. File your repatriation letter early and confirm it was received; if you still hear nothing back, that's when you call your crew manager.

Asked by sailors
"I raised an issue with the captain and it went nowhere. What's actually next?"

Straight answer: Every shipowner has its own complaint procedure: you start with your head of department, then escalate up the chain, usually ending with the captain. If that doesn't resolve it, contact your Olive Crew manager directly — that's specifically what we're here for. We can't overrule the shipowner's decision, but we raise the issue on your behalf, put it in writing, and follow up until you get an answer.

Asked by sailors
"They still haven't paid back what I spent on my medical exam. Who do I even chase this with?"

Straight answer: Reimbursement for the medical exam, gear and visa costs normally comes out of your first salary. If it's overdue, send your crew manager the amount and date — we follow up with the shipowner directly rather than leaving you to chase it yourself.

Asked by sailors
"I saw a bad review about this shipowner online before I signed. Should I have trusted it?"

Straight answer: Ask us about it before you sign — we look into specific complaints instead of dismissing them. Sometimes a review reflects one person's conflict, not a pattern; sometimes it's a real warning sign. We'll tell you honestly what we know, including when we don't have a clean answer.

Asked by sailors
"If I speak up about a problem, will that get me quietly blacklisted from future contracts?"

Straight answer: No — raising a real problem (safety, pay, contract terms) doesn't count against you with us. What does affect how a shipowner sees you is unresolved conflict with no attempt to work it out. Bring us the facts, not just the frustration, and we'll represent it accordingly.
